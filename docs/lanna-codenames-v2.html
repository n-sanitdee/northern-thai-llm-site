Layer A · lanna_khuang/ ข่วง Khuang · Open Courtyard "The open gathering space where community begins all important activity" $ lanna-khuang · formerly lannaforge · Data Foundation Toolkit
ข่วง
Khuang
"The open courtyard before a temple or palace"
Raw materials are laid out in the courtyard, organised, and prepared before any craft begins. Layer A takes the raw master Google Sheet and builds the JSONL corpus — everything downstream begins here.
$ lanna-khuang
น้ำต้นNam Ton · Water Carafe→ namton_builder.py · JSONL builder
formerly Anvil
click to expand ↗
A namton_builder.py In Lanna homes, the น้ำต้น is an earthenware pitcher with a bulbous body and slender neck. Its unglazed clay keeps water naturally cool through evaporation — a symbol of hospitality, purity, and sustainable living. Passersby could freely drink from it. This module builds the JSONL corpus from the master sheet. Like the nam ton, it takes raw water (the master sheet), cools and purifies it (validates, transforms), and pours out a clean, refreshing stream (JSONL) that all downstream layers can drink from freely. It is the first act of hospitality the benchmark offers to models and evaluators. formerly: Anvil → anvil.py images/namton.jpg
หูกHuuk · Loom→ huuk_validator.py · schema validator
formerly Loom
click to expand ↗
A huuk_validator.py The traditional Lanna floor loom for weaving textiles — threads are passed through a fixed warp, and the weaver checks every row for consistency. A single wrong thread breaks the pattern. หูก validates the JSONL schema — every row must follow the correct thread. Acts as the Loom-as-gate: no downstream layer proceeds on violations. Loom.validate() is the first call of every Layer's CLI. formerly: Loom → loom.py images/hook.jpg
ตอกTok · Bamboo Strips→ tok_splitter.py · partitioner
formerly Cleaver
click to expand ↗
A tok_splitter.py ตอก is bamboo strips. A craftsperson makes ตอก by striking along the grain with a machete, producing clean, evenly thin lengths. The action must be decisive; a hesitant split produces uneven strips that ruin the stripes. ตอก is the act of partitioning — splitting the dataset into stratified train/dev/test sets by data_type and context_dependence. The test split is locked before any model runs, never to leak. Like the craftsperson's decisive strike, this split happens once and is permanent. formerly: Cleaver → cleaver.py images/tok.jpg
รักRak · Lacquer→ rak_annotator.py · codebook applicator
formerly Quill
click to expand ↗
A rak_annotator.py Lacquer from the rak tree — applied in careful layers to coat and preserve Lanna lacquerware objects. Each coat adds depth, colour, and durability. The process is slow and deliberate. รัก applies the annotation codebook to each row, coating raw data with a durable layer of pragmatic meaning: intent, tone, construction type, code-switch flag. Each row is enriched and preserved for all downstream layers. formerly: Quill → quill.py images/rak.jpg
ตุงTung · Ritual Flag→ tung_definer.py · field definitions
formerly Compass
click to expand ↗
A tung_definer.py A Lanna Buddhist ritual banner, planted to mark direction and aspiration. Different types of ตุง (ตุงไชย, ตุงไส้หมู) carry different meanings, but all point upward — declaring the direction of intent. ตุง plants the 7 semantic flags that mark every dimension of the LannaFacets schema: intent, tone, construction_type, code_switch, data_type, context_dependence, location. Single source of truth for all field definitions across the entire codebase. formerly: Compass → compass.py images/tung.jpg

Kua Fai
"The traditional Lanna kitchen — spiritual and architectural heart of the home"

ขันโตกKhan Tok · Serving Bowl→ khantok_registry.py · 9-model registry
formerly Roster
click to expand ↗
B khantok_registry.py A traditional Lanna low-footed serving bowl used to hold multiple dishes (rice, curry, sides) on a single tray — gathering all offerings together for communal sharing. No dish is hidden; all are presented at once. ขันโตก holds the 9-model registry as a dict[str, ModelSpec]. All nine models — GPT-4o, Gemini 2.0 Flash, Claude Sonnet, DeepSeek-V3, Typhoon-S-ThaiLLM, Typhoon2, SeaLLM, Llama 3.1 8B, Qwen2.5 — are presented on one tray. The only place where model specifications live. formerly: Roster → roster.py images/khantok.jpg
ไหข้าวHai Khao · Rice Steamer→ haikhao_templater.py · prompt templating
formerly Stencil / Chalu
click to expand ↗
B haikhao_templater.py ไหข้าว is a cylindrical carved teak vessel placed over a boiling pot. It holds soaked sticky rice off the water's surface so that rising steam — not direct heat — cooks the grains evenly from below. A woven bamboo cone (วอล) inside cradles the rice and keeps it elevated. The result is fluffy, chewy sticky rice: the Lanna staple. This module is the prompt templater. It takes a soaked base template (system message + placeholders) and applies controlled "steam" (variable insertion, context filling, few-shot assembly) to produce a fully cooked prompt string — ready for the model. Like the hai khao transforms raw rice into fluffy staple food, this module transforms raw templates into prompt strings the model can digest. The only place prompts are built. formerly: Stencil → stencil.py → chalu_templater.py images/haikhao.jpg
กัวะKua · Rice Cooling Tray→ kua_inference/ · inference runner
formerly Forgehammer
click to expand ↗
B kua_inference/ · api.py + vllm.py After sticky rice is steamed in the ไหข้าว, it is spread on the กัวะ and tossed with a paddle to release steam, cool the grains, and achieve a fluffy, non-soggy texture. This quick, final step transforms hot rice into a ready-to-eat dish. กัวะ is the inference runner. It takes a fully prepared prompt (output from ไหข้าว) and passes it through the model in a single, fast forward pass — like tossing rice on the kua. No weights are updated; it simply generates outputs that are then served to the evaluation layer (จ่อฟ้า). Covers frontier APIs (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, DeepSeek) and open-weights via vLLM. formerly: Forgehammer → forgehammer/ → mui_inference/ images/kua.jpg
อั้งโล่Ang Lo · Charcoal Brazier→ anglo_finetuner.py · LoRA fine-tuner
formerly Bellows / Sin
click to expand ↗
B anglo_finetuner.py In the Lanna kitchen, the อั้งโล่ is a clay stove that burns charcoal, providing sustained, adjustable heat for slow cooking — simmering curries, stews, or making om (slow-cooked soup). It is the heart of the kitchen's heat, never rushed, always controlled. อั้งโล่ performs LoRA fine-tuning. Like the ang lo applies low, steady heat to infuse deep flavour, this module applies gradient updates over many steps to infuse dialect knowledge into the base model (Typhoon2). The heat is controlled (learning rate), never burns the base (prevents catastrophic forgetting), and produces a lightweight, portable adapter. Paper 1: PEFT + TRL, r=8, α=16, Typhoon2 only. formerly: Bellows → sin_finetuner.py images/anglo.jpg images/anglo.jpg

① Automatic Diagnostics
สาตุSatu · Affirmation→ satu_robustness.py · 3P robustness
formerly Trident
click to expand ↗
C satu_robustness.py สาตุ (สาธุ) is a Pali-derived word used as "amen" or an expression of solemn affirmation in Lanna Buddhist chanting — typically spoken or chanted three times in succession to affirm agreement with the dharma. สาตุ runs the 3P Prompt Robustness protocol, affirming three prompt variations as valid tests. Three affirmations, three prompts — each phrased differently (Thai natural / English instruct / Thai question form). Trident.PROMPTS is the single source of truth for all prompt variants. formerly: Trident → trident.py → sathu_robustness.py images/satu.jpg
ไตรภูมิTrai Phum · Three Realms→ traiphum_multiref.py · MultiRef scorer
formerly Triplet
click to expand ↗
C traiphum_multiref.py The traditional Lanna/Thai Buddhist cosmology of three realms — heaven (สวรรค์), earth (มนุษย์โลก), and underworld (นรก). Each realm is a distinct plane of existence with its own rules and inhabitants. ไตรภูมิ scores each hypothesis against three references across three realms of possible correct translation. 3 hypotheses × 3 references = 9 ChrF values per item via sacrebleu.sentence_chrf. Scores are passed to แก้ว for aggregation. formerly: Triplet → triplet.py → traiphum_multiref.py images/traiphum.jpg
แก้วKaew · Crystal→ kaew_aggregator.py · Triple-ChrF
formerly Prism
click to expand ↗
C kaew_aggregator.py Crystal or gemstone — clear, precise, and refractive. In Lanna culture, แก้ว (crystal/gem) is also a sacred term: the Buddha himself is sometimes called the "Crystal Lord." It refracts light into a full spectrum of meaning. แก้ว aggregates the 9 ChrF values from ไตรภูมิ into the Triple-ChrF trio: chrf_max (optimistic ceiling — best possible), chrf_avg (expected performance — the main metric), chrf_diff (prompt sensitivity = max − avg). Prism sorts scores before aggregating for floating-point stability. formerly: Prism → prism.py → kaew_aggregator.py images/kaew.jpg
ป่องPong · Gap / Interval→ pong_gap.py · Dialect Gap Score G
formerly Caliper
click to expand ↗
C pong_gap.py In Lanna, ป่อง refers to a gap, cleft, or interval — the space between woven bamboo slices where air passes through. The gap is not empty; it defines the structure. ป่อง computes the headline metric of the entire benchmark: G = chrf_avg(STD) − chrf_avg(NTD). This gap score measures how much harder each model finds Northern Thai dialect vs Standard Thai. A higher G means the model struggles more with dialect. Selected by highest chrf_avg on Task 1 zero-shot for the human eval selection. formerly: Caliper → caliper.py → pong_gap.py images/pong.jpg
แตะTae · Woven Bamboo Panel→ tae_slicer.py · LannaFacets slicer
formerly Lattice
click to expand ↗
C tae_slicer.py A flat woven bamboo panel made from split strips — used as fencing, walls, or a drying rack for tobacco. The gaps between woven strips allow excellent air circulation, keeping each compartment separate and ventilated. แตะ slices scores by all 7 LannaFacets metadata axes: intent, tone, construction_type, code_switch, data_type, context_dependence, location. Each gap between strips is a distinct facet cell (e.g., tone=polite_female), preventing category mixing. Emits one markdown table per facet axis per (model, condition) combination. formerly: Lattice → lattice.py → tae_slicer.py images/tae.jpg
② Human Diagnostics
ใบลานBai Lan · Palm-Leaf Manuscript→ bailan_form.py · human eval forms
formerly Slate
click to expand ↗
C bailan_form.py The traditional medium for recording Lanna scripture, legal texts, and chronicles — a blank palm leaf awaiting inscription by a scholar or monk. Once written, it is preserved for generations. ใบลาน generates human evaluation forms for annotators covering T3 (single-post response) and T4 (threaded response). Applied to the best zero-shot model + Typhoon2 LoRA. 100 items × 3 annotators, scoring Generation, Fluency, and 4 Likert axes (tone preservation, pragmatic appropriateness, naturalness, construction preservation). Output: HTML or Label Studio config. formerly: Slate → slate.py → bailan_form.py images/bailan.jpg
ฮอมปอยHom Poi · Communal Merit→ hompoi_agreement.py · Krippendorff α
formerly Concord
click to expand ↗
C hompoi_agreement.py ฮอม = to gather together and donate · ปอย = a merit-making festival. Hom Poi is the Lanna tradition of villagers pooling money, rice, or labour for a communal festival. "Who helps us, we go help them." It is the heartbeat of Lanna social solidarity. ฮอมปอย computes Krippendorff's α — measuring how well the annotator community pools their judgments into a shared reliability score. Not just whether scores match, but whether the group acts as a unified community: each annotator brings a unique offering (perspective), and the module aggregates these into a single agreement score per axis (Generation, Fluency, and 4 Likert axes). formerly: Concord → concord.py → hompoi_agreement.py images/hompoi.jpg
③ Error Analysis
กาแลKalae · Guardian Gable Finial→ kalae_error.py · error analysis
formerly Sieve / Hae
click to expand ↗
C kalae_error.py In Lanna architecture, the กาแล is a pair of carved wooden planks crossing above the roofline. It protects the home by deterring birds (physical filter) and evil spirits (spiritual guardian), while signalling the house's noble character. It stands watch, always vigilant. กาแล guards the benchmark's reliability by filtering the worst failures: worst-k rows by chrf_avg (bottom-10 per model/condition), prompt-sensitivity outliers (high chrf_diff), tone-flip cases (tone preservation ≤ 2 ∧ Generation = 1), construction-loss (CNC partition), and CD-drop (context-dependent vs independent gap). Like the kalae standing watch over the roof, this module keeps outliers from contaminating the final report. formerly: Sieve → sieve.py → hae_error.py → kalae_error.py images/kalae.jpg

อูบ
Oob
"In Lanna and Tai Khoen culture, an อูบ is a hand-crafted lacquerware container decorated with intricate gold-leaf patterns"
Used to store Buddhist manuscripts, ritual betel sets, or offerings for monks — precious, sacred items that must be preserved intact. The อูบ_meta package holds all shared utilities: schema definitions, run manifests, seed manager, I/O bridge, and orchestration. Like a ceremonial container, it safeguards the rules that ensure reproducibility.

ข้าวตอกKhao Tok · Sacred Popped Rice→ khaotok_seed.py · deterministic seed
formerly loomstone.py
click to expand ↗
meta khaotok_seed.py ข้าวตอก is made by heating unhulled paddy until it pops out of its husk — a deterministic transformation from a single grain to a light, airy piece. It is sprinkled by the lay minister (ปู่จ๋าน) in ceremonies to symbolise spreading prosperity, purity, and "blooming" luck. ข้าวตอก manages deterministic randomness. Like a single grain of rice that always pops into the same shape under the same heat, a fixed seed value produces identical random sequences across runs. seed_all(42) sprinkles randomness throughout the system — shuffling partitions, sampling dev sets, initialising model weights — ensuring reproducibility while enabling controlled variation. formerly: loomstone.py → mahin_seed.py → khaotok_seed.py images/khaotok.jpg
ปี้Pee · Tag / Mark→ pee_manifest.py · run manifest
formerly manifest.py
click to expand ↗
meta pee_manifest.py ปี้ is a mark or tag left to identify ownership or provenance — a small stamp of identity placed on an object to record where it came from, who made it, and when. Every experiment produces a ปี้: a manifest JSON stamped with config, data hashes (SHA-256), git SHA, tooling versions (python, torch, sacrebleu, vllm), and hardware. No output file is accepted by any layer unless its ปี้ resolves to a manifest on disk. Cross-run merges require --cross-run. formerly: manifest.py → pee_manifest.py images/pee.jpg
ฮีตHeet · Customary Law→ heet_schema.py · Pydantic schemas
formerly schema.py
click to expand ↗
meta heet_schema.py ฮีต (from Pali charita, meaning conduct) are the customary moral codes and traditions passed down through Lanna generations — the "right way" of living and behaving within a community. No row is accepted unless it follows the ฮีต. It is imported by every other module — the foundation of the entire type system. formerly: schema.py → heet_schema.py images/heet.jpg

The Dance in Motion
ฟ้อน · lanna-fon

The lanna-fon command is the pipeline orchestrator — it reads a YAML pipeline file and runs every layer in sequence, passing manifest IDs between stages. Think of it as the village headman (ป่อหลวง) calling the performance: each tool plays its part in order, and the benchmark runs from raw sheet to final report in one graceful, uninterrupted dance.
